**Content:**

- Incidence: 25‚Äì40% (no prophylaxis) vs 5‚Äì8% (appropriate prophylaxis)[1]

- Consequence: +7‚Äì14 days LOS, +$20‚Äì40K cost, sepsis risk[2]

**C. DIFFICILE INFECTION**
- Incidence: Increases 20‚Äì30% with each additional antibiotic day >24h[3]

- Incidence: 0.5‚Äì1% (immediate hypersensitivity)[5]
- Risk Factors: True penicillin allergy, eosinophilia, prior reaction
- Prevention: Verify allergy history; have epinephrine available[6]

**MRSA/VRE COLONIZATION**
- Incidence: Increases with prolonged antibiotic exposure[7]
- Consequence: Future infections require broader spectrum; worse outcomes
- Prevention: Strict duration limits; no "extra" doses[4]

- C. difficile: Prolonged prophylaxis causes harm, not benefit[3]
- Emphasize: "More antibiotic = worse outcomes in prophylaxis"
- Each complication reinforces: STOP at 24h, full stop

## ADDIE MICRO-CURRICULUM

### **ANALYSIS**

**Learner Gaps:**
- Many residents don't know 1-hour timing rule exists
- Common belief: "More antibiotics = safer"
- Confusion: Prophylaxis vs treatment antibiotics
- Uncertainty: Which antibiotic for which injury site

**Key Decisions:**
1. WHEN to give antibiotic (timing <1h)
2. WHAT to give (site-specific selection)
3. HOW LONG (‚â§24h, not >24h)

**Learning Level:**
- Residents: Apply-level (can implement protocol)
- Attendings: Analyze-level (can justify, troubleshoot)
- Nursing: Know-level (recognize timing, document)

---

### **DESIGN**

**Learning Objectives (Bloom's Taxonomy):**

1. **KNOW:** Recall the <1-hour timing rule and ‚â§24-hour duration limit for trauma prophylaxis. (Bloom Level 1)

2. **UNDERSTAND:** Explain WHY prophylaxis timing matters (inoculation window, bacterial adherence) and WHY prolonged courses harm patients (C. difficile, resistance). (Bloom Level 2)

3. **APPLY:** Select appropriate antibiotic for injury site (extremity = Cefazolin, CNS = Ceftriaxone) and adjust for special populations (obesity, renal impairment). (Bloom Level 3)

4. **ANALYZE:** Evaluate institutional compliance data, identify protocol violations, and recommend corrective actions. (Bloom Level 4)

5. **EVALUATE:** Critique a clinical case and defend adherence to or deviation from prophylaxis protocol based on evidence. (Bloom Level 5)
