
### 1. xABCDE Protocol: The ATLS 11th Edition Paradigm Shift  (Topic 1)
- Evolution from ABCDE → **xABCDE** with “x” = exsanguinating hemorrhage control first.[1][2][3]
- Emphasis on shock index, pulse pressure, and age-/frailty-adjusted vitals.[1][2][3]
- Incorporates permissive hypotension (SBP 80–100 mmHg) except in TBI.[4][5]

### 2. Massive Transfusion Protocols (MTP): 2024 Evidence Update  (Topic 2)
- Early MTP activation criteria, standardized triggers, and structured ratios (1:1:1).[6][7][8]
- Whole blood resuscitation vs component therapy; survival benefit with early WB.[9][10][7]
- HMGB‑1 and other markers predicting need for MTP.[11][7]

### 3. Damage Control Resuscitation (DCR) & Damage Control Surgery (DCS)  (Topic 3)
- Integration of permissive hypotension, hemostatic resuscitation, and limited crystalloids.[6][5][12]
- DCS indications, temporary abdominal closure, timing of planned re‑operation.[13][14][15][16]
- Focus on preventing/ reversing the lethal triad.[6][15]

### 4. Tranexamic Acid (TXA): Timing is Everything  (Topic 4)
- Mortality reduction when TXA given **<3 h**, maximal within 1 h.[6][17][18]
- Role in civilian vs combat trauma; integration into prehospital and ED protocols.[19][17][20][18]
- Interaction with factor concentrates and risks of late administration.[21][17][20][18]

### 7. Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI): Age‑Appropriate Management  (Topic 7)
- CPP targets (60–70 mmHg) and emerging individualized CPPopt using PRx.[22][23][24][25]
- Age‑based MAP targets and strict avoidance of permissive hypotension in TBI.[22][24][26]
- Updated ACS/Brain Trauma Foundation best‑practice guidelines.[27][26][28]

### 9. Spinal Cord Injury (SCI): Critical Care Management  (Topic 9)
- Early MAP augmentation (>85 mmHg) and choice of vasopressor.[29][30][31][32]
- Timing of decompression, neurogenic shock management, DVT prophylaxis.[29][30][31][33]
- Recent ACS/ENLS/other SCI guideline updates.[29][30][34][33][32]

### 15. Antibiotic Prophylaxis in Trauma: Tailored Timing & Duration  (Topic 15)
- AAST guidance on prophylaxis across injury patterns (open fractures, CNS, facial trauma).[35][36][37]
- Focus on **≤24 h durations** and “give within 1 h of injury/arrival.”[35][36][37][38]
- Impact of standardized protocols on time‑to‑antibiotics and infection outcomes.[35][36][37]

### 25. Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO) in Trauma  (Topic 25)
- ECMO for trauma‑associated ARDS, refractory shock, and selected TBI/burn patients.[39][40][41][42][43]
- Survival data for VV‑ECMO vs conventional ventilation in trauma cohorts.[39][40][42][44]
- Anticoagulation strategies and bleeding/neurologic complication profiles in trauma.[39][40][45][42][44]

### 26. Geriatric Trauma: Age is Not the Problem—Frailty Is  (Topic 26)
- Trauma‑Specific Frailty Index vs age/ISS for prediction of outcomes.[46][47][48][49][50]
- Geriatric triage, under‑triage issues, and ACS best‑practice guidelines in older adults.[47][49][51]
- Relationship of frailty to sepsis, pulmonary and hematologic complications, long‑term outcomes.[46][48][49][50]

### 19. Sepsis Management in Surgical ICU: SSC Bundles  (Topic 19)

### 20. Delirium in Critical Illness: Prevention, Identification, Management  (Topic 20)

### 22. Nutrition Support in Critical Illness & Trauma  (Topic 22)
- Early enteral nutrition; trauma ICU nutrition pathways.[72][73][74][75][76]
- Hypocaloric, protein‑dense strategies; timing and indications for parenteral nutrition.[72][73][74][75][76]
- Updated trauma ICU nutrition guidelines and long‑term recovery implications.[72][73][74][75][77]

### 23. Post‑Intensive Care Syndrome (PICS) & Mental Health Screening  (Topic 23)
- PTSD, anxiety, depression trajectories among ICU survivors.[78][79][80][81][82]
- Screening timing (around 1 month post‑ICU) and validated tools.[78][79][80][82][83]
- Interventions to mitigate frightening memories and improve long‑term outcomes.[78][79][80][83]
